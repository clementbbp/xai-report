François Chollet, the vice-president of Toulouse Metropole in charge of Ecology, Sustainable Development and Energy Transition, estimates on Thursday 1 September on franceinfo that with the extension of the low emission zone (ZFE) on Thursday in his metropole, "450,000 people will see a net improvement in air quality" by 2024. Several municipalities such as Marseille, Lyon and Rouen are inaugurating or extending the implementation of their low-emission zones on Thursday, targeting the oldest and most polluting vehicles. franceinfo: What are the changes in the Toulouse metropolis today? François Chollet: The EPZ is extended to trucks and vans with a Crit'Air 4 sticker. Until now, it concerned Crit'Air 5, the most polluting heavy goods vehicles. Light vehicles will only be affected as of January 1, 2023. By 2024, when the EPZ is fully operational, 45,000 vehicles will be affected, or about 16% of the fleet. We are still in an educational phase, with random checks. It is planned that the controls will be done by reading plates, with cameras, from the end of 2023 or the beginning of 2024. The order is issued for ten years. We tried to optimize the perimeter and its conditions to have the best gain in air quality and the least inconvenience for users. How can we support professionals in particular, who have not all been able to invest in a new utility vehicle? It's a constraint, so it requires anticipation. We have made sure that this EPZ is progressive in its application. This is also the reason why we have anticipated more than 18 months ago the aid for individuals and professionals. We have the possibility of making transitional exemptions on a case-by-case basis, which may be justified, for example for construction vehicles or public utility associations such as the Restos du Cœur. There is aid for the renewal of vehicles for professionals, up to 10,000 euros per vehicle. These aids are quite successful and popular. And there are negotiations on a case-by-case basis, so as to minimize the economic impact on these professionals, while not distorting the benefit of the EPZ, which is major for air quality. What are the expected effects of the EPZ? The planned modelling shows that in 2024, 450,000 people in a conurbation of 780,000 will see a net improvement in air quality. This is very important. There are nearly 8,000 people in Toulouse who are permanently above the toxicity thresholds of nitrogen oxides, this number will be reduced.
